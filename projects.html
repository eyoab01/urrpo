Finalized Projects

Tasks Done by the Urban revenue reform project office

By achieving the national vision of making the country one of the middle-income countries, cities are the centers of economic and social transition; In terms of making them serve as pillars of the next economic sector, our office is making great efforts to fulfill its role. The Office of the Urban Revenue Reform Project, in terms of its mission, is working to modernize the revenue collection of cities and improve their revenue, mainly by solving the problems of good governance in cities, and doing activities that will help to establish good governance and overcome the thinking and practice of rent-seeking.
The Urban Revenue Reform Project is being implemented in the cities of Mekele, Driredawa and Bahr Dar on a trial basis, and when it is expanded, a system will be developed to be implemented in cities across the country. It is performing.
In order to enable cities to generate revenue suitable for their development, the office has implemented the revenue reform work that was started to modernize the revenue of the cities, prepared national proclamations, regulations and strategies related to the revenue operations of the cities and presented them to the government. It is in progress.
In this regard, the office is moving broadly by focusing on the following four main work units in order to facilitate and achieve the goals of the reform mission.

IT support and implementation

  • A study document has been prepared and is in the process of being implemented in order to study and develop the Urban Land Lease Information System Development.
  • To develop a complete and organized Integrated Urban Land Holding File Management system, study work is underway by a consulting firm.
  • The implementation of a property tax information system based on geographic information in the developed property tax system is underway.
  • Implementation of property tax system has been done.
  • Network infrastructure and hardware have been supported to help implement the property tax system.
  • Computer network server hardware devices are supported
  • Research on the needs of the system for the purchase and development of a modern technology and computerized property tax system has been conducted.

Preparation of legal frameworks

  • Federal Property Transaction and Valuation Bill
  • Regional laws and regulations as well
  • Instructions and guideline documents have been prepared in draft level and are being monitored and supported for their approval.
  • Federal Real Property Marketing and Appraisal Bill Regulations and Guidelines have been prepared by the office and are being developed jointly.
  • Federal Real Estate Development Transaction and Appraisal Act; Provincial laws and regulations; Likewise, guidance and guideline documents have been prepared at a draft level and are being monitored and supported for their approval.
  • Urban development finance strategy document has been prepared and submitted to the Minister of Planning
  • Urban Revenue Collection and Management Bill has been prepared.
  • The PPP bill has been prepared
  • The preliminary draft of the city/municipal revenue management ordinance has been prepared and is in the process of collecting and developing resources.

Works related to municipal revenues

Identifying TOP-6 Own Source Revenue Types that can be a big source of income for cities has been done.

  1. Real Property Tax
  2. Urban Land Lease Payment
  3. City Business Service Fee
  4. Urban Land Permit Fee
  5. Hotel Occupancy Fee
  6. They are Road Users Fee.
  • Research works have been carried out to help prepare the legal framework for these sources of income and in the current situation
  • Urban Land Lease System Development study has been completed and put into operation in order to study the joint coordination of international and national consulting organizations.
  • Urban road service usage fee survey is being conducted in three Nabemuna cities (Bahr Dar, Adama and Dredawa).
  • The Finance Strategy Document has been prepared and sent to the concerned parties, and once approved, it is in the process of being implemented at the city level.

Capacity building activities

  • A document was prepared by a consultant to assist in the design and implementation of a public participation and mobilization program to support the property tax system.
  • A capacity building survey is underway in 24 cities to provide technology support based on the needs of the sector in order for cities to increase their revenue potential.
  • Gap-based capacity building trainings are being provided to leaders and professionals serving in the urban land and planning sector and revenue reform sector institutions.
  • Professional standards and assessment tools have been designed to enable professionals working in the income reform sector to ensure their professional competence.
  • In order to ensure the professional competence of the leadership and experts serving in the urban land and planning sector institutions, by carrying out periodic review works to ensure the professional competence of the leadership and experts, since 2006, the competence of many leaders and experts in the sector has been confirmed, and it has been possible to produce many experts in the sector. From 2006 to 2008, 4192 leaders and experts were trained in training and evaluation programs, while 1262 leaders and experts were certified in the newly revised professional standards from 2012 to 2015. In this process, a total of 7532 leaders and experts in various professions have been trained to build their capacity.
  • Capacity building that can initiate and sustain the income reform system
