About The Project Office

Establishment

For a long time, the revenue collection system of Ethiopian cities has been ineffective, prone to rent-seeking and ineffective. To support this fact with a concrete example, we can raise property tax revenue. Although the tax has been known in Ethiopia since 1944, it does not generate enough income, is not efficient, is not consistent, is not managed in a name but system, and does not give importance.
Since the attitude of rent-seeking is deeply rooted, there is a lot of activity to hide and not pay the income which is public and government wealth. In this regard, the issue that makes the problem more complicated is the fact that in addition to the act of not paying to hide income, the crime of using the bodies and documents in the bureaucracy in an organized and studied way to carry out the act or disguise its legitimacy is on the rise. This lack of thinking is seriously affecting the implementation of the laws issued by states and cities to combat illegal practices in the revenue sector.
Therefore, if our cities continue in this way and are abandoned, they will not be able to achieve the goal of becoming a center of transformation and moving from agriculture to industry. The issue of improving the system of property income and other municipal income requires special attention. The tax collection situation of Ethiopian cities is very low compared to other countries or the city's spending needs.
Although there are many reasons for this, it is widely observed that the practice of krai collection, which violates the collection systems of municipal revenues and regular revenues, and which accounts for a large share of the city's income, especially in the absence of complete information on the buyer and seller of fixed property transactions in the city, is seen when one party uses it improperly and the other is harmed. . In connection with this, brokerage work that is not guided by the legal system is seen to create a negative impact on the market because it is not guided by the knowledge and system of accurate property valuation information. In general, there is a wide gap between cities in terms of systematically collecting the revenues permitted by law and the revenues that must be collected, and identifying and collecting other high revenue sources with determination and focus, as well as the correctness of the assessment to collect the income from the property fairly, and the desired transparency and orderliness of the property market.
From this point of view, the establishment of the school office is based on;
Due to the fact that the government has paid attention to the issue of improving the revenue system of cities in order to overcome the internal and external problems mentioned above;

  • In addition to this, as mentioned above, it lays the foundation and supports the efforts started on the establishment of land and land-related registration and information cadastre for the determination and implementation of property tax;
  • The importance of working together for the successful implementation of urban development finance reform is highlighted.
    Accordingly, the Urban Revenue Reform Project Office was established at the beginning of 2009 under the Office of the Ministry of Urban Development and Housing (and now the Ministry of Urban and Infrastructure). units and organized into one supporting unit. These units are:
  • Property Tax Reform Unit
  • Municipal revenue reform unit
  • Capacity building and communication unit
  • System development and technology supply unit
  • The office is organized as having its own administrative and financial unit and it is considered to have a technical committee that monitors and supports important works.

Vision

The vision of the office is to build "prosperity center cities and a competitive construction industry" which is the vision of our ministry. It is to work to achieve the goal.

Mission

The mission of the project in general is "Improving the income system of Ethiopian cities" and in detail;

  • Take three cities (Mekele, Dredwana Bahadar) as a sample and carry out a survey of the type of income, income capacity, collection and use in the cities;
  • Based on the general principles, national policies and experience of other countries, formulating an improved revenue system for cities;
  • Develop a legal framework to implement the proposed revenue system, create capacity and empower stakeholders;
  • Implementation of the modeled revenue system; It is to summarize the experience gained from the sample implementation and expand it as a system at the national level.

In addition, three coordination units that coordinate income reform works in sample cities have been ranked in Mekelle, Bahr Dar and Dredawa cities.

General purpose

By establishing a modern, efficient and reliable revenue system based on the participation of the society and stakeholders in cities, creating the capacity for cities to play their role is to ensure good governance, stable and sustainable development that can satisfy the residents and stakeholders.

Detailed objectives

  • To combat the attitude and practice of rent-seeking around revenue collection; To create a system and capacity to help reduce and promote development.
  • Helping cities achieve fair distribution of wealth; To enhance good governance by building an efficient, transparent and accountable system and capacity.
  • Fair in cities; To enable an independent and reliable property trading and valuation system
  • By enhancing the income of cities
  • By creating an opportunity where they can afford their own expenses (from 70 to 100 percent), so that cities can gradually withdraw from the subsidy budget.
  • To enable them to provide standard infrastructure and services
  • Their ability to decide; their freedom; They are to expand their power to coordinate the people and their right to govern themselves
  • Building the capacity of urban planning and land sector institutions

Values

  • To establish honesty and accountability,
  • Ensuring effectiveness and efficiency;
  • Always follow the continuous learning and teaching process,
  • Giving priority to public interest,
  • Developing a culture of teamwork and seeing everyone as equal
  • Dealing with different ideas properly.
